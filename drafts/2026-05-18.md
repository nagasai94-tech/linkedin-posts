More than half of the entrepreneurs say they feel lonely regularly. Not remotely hidden away somewhere; but people in offices, on calls, in back-to-back meetings all day.

The problem isn't the number of touchpoints. It's the depth.

#CommunityBuilding has taught me that being in the same room or the same channel, doesn't automatically mean connection. What actually moves people is being genuinely seen and heard by the people around them.

That's what I keep coming back to when I think about what makes a #community work. Not the event count, not the member numbers or whether the people in the room feel like they belong there.

The data backs it up: 66% of entrepreneurs join a #LeadershipCommunity specifically to belong to a group of peers. Not to collect contacts.
To belong.

Running a chapter in #CorporateConnectionsAPandTS, this is the thing I pay most attention to. Anyone can fill a room. Fewer people make a room feel worth coming back to.
