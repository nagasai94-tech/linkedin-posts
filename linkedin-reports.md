### 2026-06-17 — Leadership

Most founders build teams. Fewer build #teams that can run without them.

There's a version of leadership that feels productive; you're the one in the room, the one with answers, the one who ties everything together, your team moves when you move. That looks like #effectiveness. It's actually a bottleneck.

Research on high-growth entrepreneurial firms consistently shows the same pattern: when all decisions are routed through the founder, the team stops developing independent judgment. Not because they can't, but because they don't need to.

Deepinder Goyal demonstrated this at scale. In January 2026, he stepped aside as CEO of Zomato and handed over full operational control to Aditya Mangla. He didn't just delegate a task, he designed himself out of the role entirely. The company didn't pause. That's the point.

Running a #community of entrepreneurs in Hyderabad, I see this across industries. The founders who scale well aren't the most available ones. They're the ones who've made themselves progressively less necessary for day-to-day judgment calls.

That's a different kind of discipline than showing up. It takes longer to #build. But it's the only thing that compounds.
